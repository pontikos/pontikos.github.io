Nikolas Pontikos

 View Nikolas Pontikos's profile I'm currently a postdoc at University College London working with Dr Vincent Plagnol and collaborating with various groups interested in identifying variants in rare clinical phenotypes. Here is the current list of groups I'm working with:
  • Professor Tony Segal, based in the Raynes building, senior PI and clinician interested in neutrophil biology and inflammatory bowel disorder in which neutrophils play a key role. He is particularly interested in manifestations of the disease in the Ashkenazi Jewish population.
  • Professor Alison Hardcastle at the Institute of Ophthalmology.
I did my Phd with Dr Chris Wallace . I worked on normalisation and clustering methods applied to flow cytometry datasets. I have written some software to analyse flow datasets which you can find on github. I also had the opportunity of working on some genetic data in particular I devised a technique for imputing the copy number of two KIR genes from SNP signals. You can find my thesis here.
